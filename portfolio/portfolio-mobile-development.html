Mobile Application Market Overview

Since the launch of Apple's iPhone, the introduction of the iOS mobile operating system, and the opening of Apple’s App Store, mobile applications have exploded in popularity. Though Apple remains the market's pace car in terms of innovation, the race for mobile app market share has quickly expanded: Google, Palm and Research In Motion (RIM; makers of Blackberry) have each developed app stores for their respective operating systems and mobile hardware platforms.


Businesses have used mobile applications as sales tools, connecting clients and potential customers to their products and services faster, and more cost-effectively, than ever before. Mobile applications have also been used to mobilize brands and keep companies' names and brand identities at the front of people's minds. This communicates the value of your brand in a big way.

The 2010 introduction of the iPad running iOS has popularized the tablet computer market, and in so doing has made it possible to develop mobile apps for a platform that is even more versatile and more powerful than even the most advanced mobile phones. A recent study by consultancy firm Gartner places the total yearly value of the market for mobile apps at $58 billion within three years—a rapid year-over-year growth from last year's comparatively paltry 8.2 billion downloads.
